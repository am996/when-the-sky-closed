# 🗝️ WHEN THE SKY CLOSED: MASTER KEY 🗝️

This document contains the comprehensive logic and solutions for the "When the Sky Closed" ARG. Use this to guide testing or assist stuck players.

---

## 🧠 Canon Design Spine

The ARG is now **story-gated**, not merely puzzle-gated.

Players should progress because they understand the worlds thematically:

> Reality only survives when contradiction and imperfection are allowed to exist.

The Time Keeper is teaching contradiction tolerance, instability acceptance, and identity persistence. She believes perfect consistency destroys individuality because total alignment collapses separation.

### Phase 1 Purpose
Phase 1 remains beautiful, surreal, and mostly safe. Each node teaches a survival principle:

*   **Candella:** identity can persist through change.
*   **Verdant Rail:** truth sometimes requires seeing the wrong path first.
*   **Glasshollow:** contradiction can still contain truth.
*   **Rain Market:** meaning can survive drift without becoming fixed.
*   **Final Gate:** paradox can hold together if imperfection is preserved.

### Phase 2 Canon
Phase 2 is existential/psychological horror, not gore or chase horror. The horror should escalate slowly through malfunction:

*   old nodes retroactively mutate,
*   Frog Notes rewrite,
*   archive text changes,
*   echoes appear before interaction,
*   meanings invert,
*   systems remember incorrectly.

### Researcher Fragments
Researchers from the pregame are now integrated as **fragmented consciousnesses**, not ghosts. They are incomplete observer states, partially aligned humans trapped between realities.

Failure modes:

*   **Identity Erosion:** loses names/pronouns and cannot maintain self-definition.
*   **Observer Corruption:** remembers player actions before they happen.
*   **Emotional Inversion:** describes comfort fearfully and horror calmly.
*   **Temporal Looping:** repeats recursive dialogue with slight variations.
*   **Sky Witness Fragment:** closest to the truth; describes stars folding inward, horizons sealing, and separation disappearing.

Frog is emotionally central. Future reveals should imply he may have abandoned the researchers before the sky fully closed.

## 📍 Node 1: Candella (The Parade)
**Concept:** Stability within change. Players often try to fix the "order" of the parade, but the world asks what remains the same when everything else shifts.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Discovery:** You must click every fragment at least **2 times** to observe how they change.
*   **Persistence:** You must reach **12 total clicks** before the Frog realizes: *"It's asking what stayed itself."*
*   **Evidence Gate:** The selection only works after Frog has notes for all selected stable fragments **and** both unstable fragments.
*   **Observation:** The selected fragments must each have generated a Frog Note. The point is evidence, not grind.
*   **UI Clarity:** Frog's Notes now explicitly remind players to revisit fragments and watch what keeps its role.

### ✅ Solution
Select the three **Stable** fragments:
1.  **Candy Gate**
2.  **Confetti Bridge**
3.  **Final Arch**
*(Leave "Laughter Cart" and "Broken Music Wagon" unchecked as they are unstable).*

## 📍 Node 2: Verdant Rail (The Archive)
**Concept:** Directional truth. The archive is "slippery"—reading out of order or skipping pages causes the document to reset or provide false information.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Sequence Locking:** Each section only reveals its "true" text if the previous one was read immediately before it.
*   **The Harbor Trap:** The "Station Index" will mention a "Harbor" station if read incorrectly. Harbor does not belong to this timeline.
*   **Scar Requirement:** The archive must record at least **2 wrong-path scars** before it accepts the route. This prevents immediately typing the answer without observing failure.
*   **The Confirmation Pass:** Simply knowing the order isn't enough. Once you type the route, you must demonstrate it by clicking the tabs in that exact order one last time.

### ✅ Solution
1.  First, produce at least **2 wrong-path scars** by reading sections in broken orders.
2.  Then click **System Overview**.
3.  Click **Anomaly Log**.
4.  Click **Station Index**.
5.  Click **Transit Behavior Report**.
6.  In the text box that appears, type: `overview anomaly index transit`.
7.  **Crucial:** Click the tabs again in that exact order: **Overview -> Anomaly -> Index -> Transit**.

## 📍 Node 3: Glasshollow (The Echoes)
**Concept:** Saturation. Glasshollow doesn't want you to solve it; it wants you to exist within it until the reflections "catch up."

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Randomized Echoes:** Echoes trigger on a random delay (3.5s to 7.7s) after clicking a shard.
*   **Corruption:** Every click and every echo increases the "Corruption Level."
*   **Collision Rule:** Clicking before the delayed echo arrives creates an Echo Collision. It gives atmosphere, but does not progress the node.
*   **Requirement:** You must see at least **8 echoes**, reach **Corruption Level 12**, and resolve at least **2 echoes per shard**.

### ✅ Solution
1.  Click all four shards (**Room, Safe, Memory, Frog**) at least **2 times** each.
2.  **Wait after each click.** Clicking before the echo arrives creates an echo collision and does not help progress.
3.  Let at least **8 echoes** resolve, including at least **2 resolved echoes per shard**.
4.  Continue until the Time Keeper says: *"Echo saturation complete."*

## 📍 Node 4: Rain Market (The Trade)
**Concept:** Meaning Drift. In the Rain Market, words lose their permanence the more you use them. You must trade until the language is fully "broken."

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Inspection:** Clicking a stall only inspects it. Progress requires choosing **Keep Meaning** or **Trade Meaning** after inspection.
*   **Global Drift:** Every keep/trade decision increases market drift.
*   **Sampling:** Every item must be inspected and meaning-shifted at least once.
*   **Evidence Gate:** Stable items must be both kept and traded at least once. Unstable items must be traded at least twice.
*   **Goal:** Reach **Global Drift 16** while satisfying the evidence gates.

### ✅ Solution
1.  Inspect every stall.
2.  For **Path, Certainty, and Repetition**, choose both **Keep Meaning** and **Trade Meaning** at least once.
3.  For **Beginning, Sound, and Shelter**, choose **Trade Meaning** at least twice.
4.  Continue decisions until every item has shifted and the **Drift Level** reaches **16**.

## 📍 Node 5: The Final Gate (Alignment)
**Concept:** The Paradox Solution. The central gate cannot be synchronized with "Perfect Consistency." It requires a state that accepts the broken nature of the timelines.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Story Gate:** The gate checks that the Phase 1 insights have been earned. Correct dropdowns alone are not enough.
*   **The Consistency Trap:** Choosing all "Stable" or "Fixed" options will cause the Frog to reject the solution as "too clean" to hold.
*   **Paradox Requirement:** The gate must be set to the **Drifting/Contradictory** options.
*   **Witness Phrase:** Dropdowns alone are not enough. The player must enter a phrase containing the surviving concepts: `start`, `path`, `glasshollow`, and `drift`.
*   **Near-Miss Requirement:** The gate must experience at least **1 near-paradox failure** before it accepts the true collapse. A near miss is any configuration with 3 of the 4 paradox locks correct.
*   **Repeated Confirmation:** The gate requires at least **3 total collapse submissions** and at least one near miss before it accepts the true configuration.

### ✅ Solution
Set the dropdowns to these exact values:
*   **Candella System:** `stable_inside_drift`
*   **Railway Alignment:** `wrong_station`
*   **Echo Synchronization:** `accepted_contradiction`
*   **Memory Core:** `drifting`

Witness phrase examples:
*   `start path glasshollow drift`
*   `the start, the path, glasshollow, and drift survived`

Before the accepted submission, create at least one near miss with 3 of the 4 lock concepts correct.

Typical path: one near miss, then submit the true final configuration twice.
