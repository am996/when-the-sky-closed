# 🗝️ WHEN THE SKY CLOSED: MASTER KEY 🗝️

This document contains the comprehensive logic and solutions for the "When the Sky Closed" ARG. Use this to guide testing or assist stuck players.

---

## 🧠 Canon Design Spine

The ARG is now **story-gated**, not merely puzzle-gated.

Players should progress because they understand the worlds thematically:

> Reality only survives when contradiction and imperfection are allowed to exist.

The Time Keeper is teaching contradiction tolerance, instability acceptance, and identity persistence. She believes perfect consistency destroys individuality because total alignment collapses separation.

### Phase 1 Purpose
Phase 1 remains beautiful, surreal, and mostly safe. Each node teaches a survival principle:

*   **Candella:** identity can persist through change.
*   **Verdant Rail:** truth sometimes requires seeing the wrong path first.
*   **Glasshollow:** contradiction can still contain truth.
*   **Rain Market:** meaning can survive drift without becoming fixed.
*   **Final Gate:** paradox can hold together if imperfection is preserved.

### Phase 2 Canon
Phase 2 is existential/psychological horror, not gore or chase horror. The horror should escalate slowly through malfunction:

*   old nodes retroactively mutate,
*   Frog Notes rewrite,
*   archive text changes,
*   echoes appear before interaction,
*   meanings invert,
*   systems remember incorrectly.

Phase 2 now continues in-game after the Phase 1 gate release. The systems no longer behave like separate worlds. HERTA, researcher fragments, Frog's memory, and the Time Keeper's alignment logic begin folding into one another.

### Phase 2 Play Order
1.  **HERTA Wakes:** the archive starts behaving like an active contamination source.
2.  **HERTA Contamination Index:** open researcher remnants and submit the containment thesis.
3.  **Encoded Signal / Spectrogram Gate:** enter the external archive URL.
4.  **Researcher Recording:** recover the final refusal phrase.
5.  **Observer Collapse:** Frog understands why fractured memory preserved him.
6.  **Containment Choice / Final Seal:** choose Frog, enter the refusal, and manually activate containment.

### Phase 2 Skip Check
In normal play, Phase 2 cannot be skipped:

*   The HERTA archive scene only advances after the containment thesis is accepted.
*   The spectrogram scene only advances after the decoded Google Drive folder link is submitted.
*   The Final Seal appears only after **Containment Choice** in normal play.

The final seal phrase alone is not enough unless the player is already on the Final Seal screen. If the game has not unlocked containment, Frog refuses with: `No... not yet. We have to understand what containment means before we choose it.`

### Researcher Fragments
Researchers from the pregame are now integrated as **fragmented consciousnesses**, not ghosts. They are incomplete observer states, partially aligned humans trapped between realities.

Failure modes:

*   **Identity Erosion:** loses names/pronouns and cannot maintain self-definition.
*   **Observer Corruption:** remembers player actions before they happen.
*   **Emotional Inversion:** describes comfort fearfully and horror calmly.
*   **Temporal Looping:** repeats recursive dialogue with slight variations.
*   **Sky Witness Fragment:** closest to the truth; describes stars folding inward, horizons sealing, and separation disappearing.

Frog is emotionally central. Future reveals should imply he may have abandoned the researchers before the sky fully closed.

Frog ultimately survives alignment exposure because his memory fractured before it could fully converge. His incompleteness preserves individuality. This makes him the only viable final vessel.

## 📍 Node 1: Candella (The Parade)
**Concept:** Stability within change. Players often try to fix the "order" of the parade, but the world asks what remains the same when everything else shifts.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Discovery:** You must click every fragment at least **2 times** to observe how they change.
*   **Persistence:** You must reach **12 total clicks** before the Frog realizes: *"It's asking what stayed itself."*
*   **Evidence Gate:** The selection only works after Frog has notes for all selected stable fragments **and** both unstable fragments.
*   **Observation:** The selected fragments must each have generated a Frog Note. The point is evidence, not grind.
*   **Concrete UI Goal:** Frog's Notes show progress toward 5/5 notes and explicitly tell players to choose exactly three fragments whose role stayed the same.

### ✅ Solution
Select the three **Stable** fragments:
1.  **Candy Gate**
2.  **Confetti Bridge**
3.  **Final Arch**
*(Leave "Laughter Cart" and "Broken Music Wagon" unchecked as they are unstable).*

## 📍 Node 2: Verdant Rail (The Archive)
**Concept:** Directional truth. The archive is "slippery"—reading out of order or skipping pages causes the document to reset or provide false information.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Sequence Locking:** Each section only reveals its "true" text if the previous one was read immediately before it.
*   **Non-Tab Route:** The true path is intentionally **not** the visible left-to-right button order.
*   **The Harbor Trap:** The "Station Index" will mention a "Harbor" station if read incorrectly. Harbor does not belong to this timeline.
*   **Scar Requirement:** The archive must record at least **2 wrong-path scars** before it accepts the route. This prevents immediately typing the answer without observing failure.
*   **The Confirmation Pass:** Simply knowing the order isn't enough. Once you type the route, you must demonstrate it by clicking the tabs in that exact order one last time.
*   **Concrete UI Goal:** Frog's Sequence Notes show scar progress, the current path, and dependency clues.

### ✅ Solution
1.  First, produce at least **2 wrong-path scars** by reading sections in broken orders.
2.  Then click **Anomaly Log**.
3.  Click **System Overview**.
4.  Click **Transit Behavior Report**.
5.  Click **Station Index**.
6.  In the text box that appears, type: `anomaly overview transit index`.
7.  **Crucial:** Click the tabs again in that exact order: **Anomaly -> Overview -> Transit -> Index**.

### 🧠 Why This Is The Route
*   **Anomaly Log first:** the archive must admit the break before any overview can be trusted.
*   **System Overview second:** the map becomes truthful only after the flaw is named.
*   **Transit third:** movement stabilizes before labels do.
*   **Station Index last:** station names are only trustworthy after the route is already moving.

## 📍 Node 3: Glasshollow (Mirror Accord)
**Concept:** Contradiction containment. Glasshollow no longer asks players to wait for echoes. It asks them to actively choose which reflections can disagree without destroying each other.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Shard Cycling:** Each shard cycles through three readings when clicked.
*   **One Anchor:** The Room shard must remain plain and literal: `We are in Glasshollow.`
*   **Three Accords:** Safety, Memory, and Frog must be set to readings that preserve both sides of a contradiction.
*   **Hold Reflection:** After observing every shard at least once, players press **Hold Reflection** to test the mirror state.
*   **Failure Feedback:** Wrong holds explain whether the player lost the anchor or forced a shard into a too-absolute answer.

### ✅ Solution
Set the shards to:
1.  **Room:** `Anchor` / `We are in Glasshollow.`
2.  **Safety:** `Accord` / `This place can frighten us and still hold us.`
3.  **Memory:** `Accord` / `This memory can be mine and not mine.`
4.  **Frog:** `Accord` / `Frog can remember both directions and remain Frog.`

Then click **Hold Reflection**.

### 🧠 Why This Works
Glasshollow teaches that contradiction is not automatically corruption. One truth must stay named, but the other memories survive by allowing two incompatible feelings to coexist.

## 📍 Node 4: Rain Market (The Trade)
**Concept:** Meaning Drift. In the Rain Market, words lose their permanence the more you use them. The concrete task is to test all six concepts, then select the three whose emotional role survived both keeping and trading.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Inspection:** Clicking a stall only inspects it. Progress requires choosing **Keep Meaning** or **Trade Meaning** after inspection.
*   **Trade Ledger:** The UI now shows kept/traded counts and drift per concept.
*   **Sampling:** Every item must drift at least once.
*   **Evidence Gate:** Path, Certainty, and Repetition must each be both kept and traded at least once. Beginning, Sound, and Shelter must each be traded at least once.
*   **Selection Unlock:** Reach **10 total decisions** while satisfying the evidence gates to unlock **Meaning Selection**.
*   **Concrete Final Task:** Choose exactly three concepts whose emotional role survived the exchanges.

### ✅ Solution
1.  Inspect every stall.
2.  For **Path, Certainty, and Repetition**, choose both **Keep Meaning** and **Trade Meaning** at least once.
3.  For **Beginning, Sound, and Shelter**, choose **Trade Meaning** at least once.
4.  Continue decisions until every item has shifted and the **Drift Level** reaches **10**.
5.  In **Meaning Selection**, select:
    **A path that may not exist**, **A moment of certainty**, and **A broken repetition**.

## 📍 Node 5: The Final Gate (Alignment)
**Concept:** The Paradox Solution. The central gate cannot be synchronized with "Perfect Consistency." It requires a state that accepts the broken nature of the timelines.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Story Gate:** The gate checks that the Phase 1 insights have been earned. Correct dropdowns alone are not enough.
*   **The Consistency Trap:** Choosing all "Stable" or "Fixed" options will cause the Frog to reject the solution as "too clean" to hold.
*   **Paradox Requirement:** The gate must be set to the **Drifting/Contradictory** options.
*   **Witness Phrase:** Dropdowns alone are not enough. The player must enter a phrase containing the surviving concepts: `start`, `path`, `glasshollow`, and `drift`.
*   **Concrete UI Goal:** The gate now accepts the correct configuration immediately once the four world insights and witness phrase are present. Near-miss and repeated-submit requirements were removed because they felt arbitrary.

### ✅ Solution
Set the dropdowns to these exact values:
*   **Candella Lock:** choose `Hold anchors inside drift` (`stable_inside_drift`)
*   **Rail Lock:** choose `Route with a foreign station` (`wrong_station`)
*   **Mirror Lock:** choose `Accept the contradiction` (`accepted_contradiction`)
*   **Memory Lock:** choose `Let meaning drift` (`drifting`)

Witness phrase examples:
*   `start path glasshollow drift`
*   `the start, the path, glasshollow, and drift survived`

Submit once after all four previous insights have been earned.

## 📍 Phase 2: HERTA Contamination Index
**Concept:** The HERTA archive is alive as a contamination source. Players do not replace the original HERTA document; they examine later remnants that show how each researcher failed.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Researcher Files:** Six contaminated records appear in the in-game archive.
*   **Linked Remnants:** Each record links to a plain-text file under `archive/remnants/`.
*   **Failure Modes:** Players must read enough files to understand how observer collapse works.
*   **Story Gate:** The thesis must include the core idea: contradiction/disagreement protects identity/separation, and alignment must be contained/sealed.
*   **Frog Recognition:** Frog begins recognizing the researchers through failure patterns, not names. This is intentional: his incomplete memory is evidence that he survived partial alignment.

### ✅ Solution
Open at least **5 of 6** researcher files, then submit a thesis containing:
*   `contradiction` or `disagreement`
*   `identity`, `separate`, or `individual`
*   `contain` or `seal`

Example accepted thesis:
`contradiction protects identity, so alignment must be contained`

*   `archive/remnants/01_chatlog_observer-delay.txt` — Observer Corruption

*   `archive/remnants/03_failed_transcription_identity.txt` — Identity Erosion

## 📍 Phase 2: Encoded Signal / Spectrogram Gate
**Concept:** The archive points beyond itself. Players must decode an audio-shaped clue into a hidden external archive path, then bring that path back into the browser experience.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   The spectrogram scene appears after the HERTA contamination thesis is accepted.
*   The looping tone is only a lightweight in-browser placeholder; the puzzle UI treats the decoded result as a URL.
*   This gate should feel like an investigation handoff, not a cryptography wall.
*   Current code accepts any pasted text that contains both `drive.google.com` and the folder ID `1lfXAMho3-IccE5l_mpiSult5_WJ-m58o`.
*   This means the raw URL, a markdown link, or a Drive URL without the `?usp=drive_link` suffix should all work on a fresh load.

### ✅ Solution
Preferred answer to submit:

`https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1lfXAMho3-IccE5l_mpiSult5_WJ-m58o?usp=drive_link`

Minimum required text in the submitted answer:

*   `drive.google.com`
*   `1lfXAMho3-IccE5l_mpiSult5_WJ-m58o`

If a team is already on an older loaded page, use the full raw URL exactly, not markdown.

After the link is accepted, the game continues to the local **Researcher Recording** scene.

### Researcher Recording Key
The recording reveals the final refusal phrase:

`Seperate differences reduced contradiction.`

This phrase is the required answer for the later Final Seal. It also confirms the main Phase 2 truth: contradictions were not flaws, they were barriers that preserved separation.

## 📍 Phase 2: Observer Collapse
**Concept:** Researcher fragments become more coherent, and that coherence is frightening. The horror is no longer distortion alone; it is the loss of difference.

### 🛠️ Mechanics / Story Beats
*   Frog recognizes the researchers emotionally before he remembers them factually.
*   The fragments imply: `You left before the sky finished closing.`
*   Frog realizes his fractured memory preserved his individuality.
*   The Time Keeper remains calm and merciful-sounding. She believes coherence is rest.

### Intended Interpretation
Frog did not survive because he was stronger than the researchers. He survived because he broke before alignment could finish making him coherent.

## 📍 Final Seal: Containment
**Concept:** The Time Keeper cannot be destroyed. Perfect understanding strengthens alignment. The only remaining option is containment.

### 🛠️ Mechanics
*   **Player Responsibility:** The player must choose the vessel and activate the final seal manually.
*   **Correct Vessel:** Frog.
*   **Why Frog:** fractured memory, incomplete identity, contradiction tolerance, partial alignment survival.
*   **Final Refusal:** The answer is a phrase, not an explanation. Players must bring forward the sentence preserved by the researcher recording.
*   **Realization Interlude:** Preparing the seal plays Frog's realization scene first. Only after that scene ends does the game return to the Final Seal panel for manual activation.
*   **Tone:** quiet, still, merciful, devastating. Not a battle.

### ✅ Solution
1.  Choose **Frog** as the vessel.
2.  In the final refusal field, enter: `seperate differences reduced contradiction`.
3.  Click **Prepare Seal**.
4.  Read Frog's realization sequence.
5.  When the game returns to the Final Seal panel, click **Activate Final Seal**.

### Final Cutscene Trigger
What actually triggers the ending sequence:

1.  The player must have reached the Final Seal through Phase 2 and **Containment Choice**. The phrase alone will not work early.
2.  **Prepare Seal** with:
    *   Vessel: `Frog`
    *   Final refusal: `seperate differences reduced contradiction`
3.  This starts Frog's realization cutscene, **The Sky Closed**.
4.  After that scene ends and the Final Seal panel returns, click **Activate Final Seal**.
5.  That click starts the final ending cutscene, **The Sky Closed - Final Moments**.

The phrase is normalized by the game, so capitalization and punctuation do not matter.

Accepted Final Seal phrases on the current pushed build:

*   `seperate differences reduced contradiction`
*   `separate differences reduced contradiction`
*   `let them remain different`
*   Any phrase containing `differences`, `reduced`, `contradiction`, and either `seperate` or `separate`

If a team is already on an older loaded page, use the exact phrase:

`seperate differences reduced contradiction`

### Drive Note
The Google Drive is not embedded as a visible page. The website asks players to type the decoded Drive folder URL into the Encoded Signal / Spectrogram Gate. Once the accepted Drive link/folder ID is submitted, the browser experience continues into the local Researcher Recording scene.

The final scene ends with:

`Thanks to you, my friend...`

`...the sky is finally closed at peace.`

### Ambiguity To Preserve
Do not explain whether Frog saved reality, ended it, trapped the Time Keeper, or became part of alignment. The correct ending feeling is peaceful, sad, and unresolved.
